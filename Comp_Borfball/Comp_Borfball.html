Background Info

Borfball is a competitive 5 player vs. 5 player game. To win, a team must knock over a stack of bricks located behind the opposing team. There are quite a few rules, but you only need to worry about the fact that a game of borfball is divided into two parts.

In the first part, called the laning phase, the players generally stay in their positions. Opposing players usually compete against each other individually, occasionally leaving their positions to help teammates.

In the second part, called the late game, the players usually group up to try to overpower the opposition together, although there may still be occasional 1 vs. 1 battles.

Teams that excel in the laning phase benefit from pushing to win quickly. Teams that excel in the late game benefit from dragging the game out. Some teams may even excel in both phases.

Summary of Key Terms

Laning phase: early in the game; teams that excel in this phase benefit from pushing to win quickly

Late game: late in the game; teams that excel in this phase benefit from dragging the game out

Note: It is possible for a borfball team to excel in both phases.
